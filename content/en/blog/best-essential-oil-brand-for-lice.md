## Why essential oils work against head lice

The head louse (Pediculus humanus capitis) breathes through openings called spiracles, located along its abdomen. Effective natural treatments exploit this weakness: a fatty base (coconut oil, apricot kernel oil, plant oil esters) coats the parasite and blocks its airways within minutes. This is a mechanical action, against which lice cannot develop resistance, unlike chemical insecticides.

**Essential oils** complete this mechanism. Tea tree (Melaleuca alternifolia) and lavender or lavandin provide a documented antiparasitic action and help detach nits from the hair shaft. The [tea tree essential oil benefits](/en/blog/tea-tree-essential-oil-benefits/) are not limited to lice: it is one of the most studied oils in the family aromatherapy kit.

The efficacy of the tea tree and lavender duo was measured in a reference clinical trial conducted in Australia on 123 infested children.

> "The percentage of subjects who were louse-free one day after the last treatment was 97.6% (41/42) with the melaleuca oil and lavender oil product, compared with 25.0% (10/40) with the pyrethrins and piperonyl butoxide product."
> Barker and Altman, BMC Dermatology, 2010

This fourfold gap is largely explained by the growing resistance of lice to pyrethrins, while the suffocating action and essential oils retain their efficacy. The same team confirmed in 2011 the ovicidal action of the melaleuca and lavender combination on nits.

## Puressentiel, Compagnie des Sens, Aroma-Zone: the credible alternatives

None of these three brands is a bad choice. The difference lies in the level of proof, the format and the user's degree of autonomy.

Puressentiel, present in pharmacies since 2005, offers the best clinically documented anti-lice lotion on the natural market: an efficacy and tolerance study conducted double-blind under dermatological control on 43 infested patients, with a 15-minute application time. The 100% natural formula, free of neurotoxic insecticide, combines suffocating plant oils with soothing essential oils. The steel comb with micro-fluted teeth is included. What is missing is full organic certification and an integrated shampoo, which places the brand just behind Aromapoux.

Compagnie des Sens, founded in Lyon in 2014, does not sell a ready-to-use lice kit but publishes detailed protocols based on lavandin super and tea tree, with AB-certified oils and chromatographic analyses available online. It is the most transparent option for preparing your own blend, provided the dilutions in a carrier oil are followed scrupulously.

Aroma-Zone plays the price card: its lavandin super and tea tree essential oils, mostly organic, cost 2 to 5 euros per 10 ml. A homemade anti-lice blend thus comes to less than 10 euros, comb not included. The trade-off is the absence of a ready-made formula and full responsibility for dosing. In both DIY cases, choosing [quality organic essential oils](/en/blog/where-to-buy-organic-essential-oils/), chemotyped and traced, determines the result.

## How to use an essential oil lice treatment

Whatever the chosen brand, the effective protocol follows three inseparable steps. A treatment applied without thorough combing fails in most cases, because nits survive the product.

1. **Saturating application**: apply the product strand by strand on dry hair, until the scalp and lengths are completely saturated. Respect the indicated application time (15 minutes for the Puressentiel lotion, daily application for 2 to 3 days for Aromapoux).
2. **Systematic combing**: run the fine-toothed lice comb strand by strand, from root to tip, wiping the comb after each pass. This mechanical step removes dead lice, larvae and nits.
3. **Renewal**: reapply the treatment a few days later (on day 3 for Puressentiel, until disappearance for Aromapoux) to eliminate lice hatched in the meantime, then check the head for two weeks.

For prevention, a few drops of true lavender or lavandin behind the ears and on the neck, or diluted in a hair spray, create a recognised repellent barrier before school. The logic is the same as for the [natural mosquito repellent spray brands](/en/blog/natural-mosquito-repellent-spray-best-brands/): the scent creates an environment unfavourable to the parasite without killing it.

## Precautions: a natural treatment is not a harmless treatment

Essential oils are active concentrates. Anti-lice treatments containing them, including Aromapoux and the Puressentiel lotion, are reserved for children over 3 years of age. They are not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women or for children with asthma or epilepsy without medical advice. Before first use, a skin test in the crook of the elbow, 48 hours before application, rules out an allergic reaction.

For an infant or a child under 3, the only safe approach remains mechanical: combing on damp hair coated with conditioner, repeated every 2 to 3 days for two weeks. In case of doubt, scalp superinfection or failure after two properly conducted treatments, the advice of a pharmacist or doctor is required.

### The 3 mistakes to avoid

1. Using a pure essential oil directly on a child's scalp: oils are always diluted in a fatty base, and ready-to-use formulas exist precisely to secure this dosage.
2. Skipping the fine-toothed comb step: no product, natural or chemical, replaces strand-by-strand combing to remove nits.
3. Treating the whole family as a precaution: only infested heads are treated. On the other hand, hats, brushes, pillowcases and soft toys go in the washing machine at 60°C or in quarantine for 48 hours in a sealed bag.
